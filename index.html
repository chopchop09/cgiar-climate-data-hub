Vision & roadmap

Shared, trusted infrastructure for climate decisions

By 2030, the Commons aims to connect CGIAR's distributed climate data to the decisions that affect smallholder farmers, pastoralists and fishers. Its distinctive role is the translation layer: turning raw climate data into actionable, decision-ready insight.

Working with the datasets directly?

The Commons is the general entry point: what climate data exists across CGIAR, which use cases it serves, and where to find it. If you are a data scientist who needs the catalogue itself, the Data Hub is the layer below this one, a subset of the Commons.

  • A searchable, openly licensed catalogue, quality-assured with automated checks on submission
  • Open metadata standards (STAC, schema.org, OGC) and FAO/UN vocabularies, interoperable with NASA, ESA and others
  • ARCO, analysis-ready cloud-optimised access: query data subsets without downloading terabytes
  • Runnable tutorials and dataset documentation
  • The full use-case portfolio, with champions, coordinators and action plans
  • A contribution path for adding data or docs, and agent skills for AI tooling

Common questions about the Commons

A single portal that turns authoritative CGIAR and partner climate data into decision-ready evidence, organised around the use cases it serves. It curates and links to trusted sources and complements national data systems; it does not re-host or replace existing platforms. The Data Hub is its technical layer, the metadata, tools and infrastructure that underpin the use cases, while the Commons around it brings in the wider CGIAR climate evidence and expertise those use cases draw on.
The Commons links to the original source platform for each dataset. Downloads, licences and access conditions are governed by each source. Attribution is visible on every dataset card.
This feature is still a prototype: the controls are in place but no document is generated yet. As designed, you select a country, audience and topic focus, then a page length, and the system assembles a brief from the latest CGIAR evidence for you to review and edit before exporting to PDF.
Currency varies by dataset and is displayed on each source card. The news widget is automatically compiled from monitored sources and editorially curated. Ask the Hub draws on CGIAR publications only. FAO and broader partner sources are planned.
That is the design, and the citation list is shown in the prototype, but Ask the Hub is not yet connected to a retrieval back end: the current answer is a fixed sample and its sources are illustrative. Once connected it will cite CGIAR sources only, with FAO and broader partner sources planned. Answers will not replace expert advice or peer review.
Use the contact form or email the Commons team. Datasets are reviewed against CGIAR data quality standards before being added, with attribution and complete metadata required. Contributing puts your data in front of other CGIAR programmes that can reuse it, keeps your centre's attribution visible, and can seed co-developed analytical products; over time, tracking how often data is accessed and cited helps show its reach. Persistent citable identifiers (DOIs) are being explored as one way to credit that reuse.

The CGIAR Climate Commons is a curated portal that turns trusted climate and agricultural data into decision-ready evidence. It complements national data systems and existing platforms rather than replacing them.
