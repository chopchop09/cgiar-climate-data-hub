The use cases the Commons serves

The Climate Commons exists to connect CGIAR teams to the climate and agricultural data they need. This page lists the use cases currently in the portfolio, what each is trying to achieve, who is championing it, and where it has reached. Each has a downloadable brief.

Cross-cutting

Gender, equity and social inclusion

Climate risks are not socially neutral. Women, youth and marginalised groups often face the sharpest exposure and the greatest barriers to acting on it, yet socially-disaggregated evidence is the most fragmented of all. A gender equality and social inclusion (GESI) layer runs across the Commons: integrating social-vulnerability and gender-disaggregated data so that analysis shows not only where climate risk is highest, but who is most affected, and where action can reduce risk and inequality together. It brings together the evidence, methods and indicators teams need to build that lens into their work.

For review. Developed with CGIAR's gender and social-inclusion focal points; specifics to be confirmed before external use.
